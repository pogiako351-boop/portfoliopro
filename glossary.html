AER - Annual Equivalent Rate

AER shows the actual annual interest rate you’ll earn if interest is compounded. It’s the standard way UK banks advertise savings rates so you can compare fairly. If a bank pays 4% AER monthly, you earn interest on your interest every month.

APR - Annual Percentage Rate

APR is used for loans and credit cards. It includes the interest rate plus most fees, giving you the total yearly cost of borrowing. A loan with 5% interest and £100 fee will have a higher APR than 5%.

Compound Interest

Compound interest means you earn interest on your original deposit plus the interest you’ve already earned. Over time this creates exponential growth. The earlier you start saving, the more compound interest works for you.

FSCS Protection

The Financial Services Compensation Scheme protects up to £85,000 per person, per bank in the UK if the bank fails. Check that your bank is FSCS protected before depositing large amounts.
